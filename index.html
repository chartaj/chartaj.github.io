Abstract

The scarcity of affordable, quality chart ⟨instruction, data, code, image⟩ quadruplets continues to impede advancements in large language model (LLM)-generated charts. In response, we introduce two novel contributions: ChartAJ, the first effective reference-free auto-judge tailored towards chart generation feedback, and ChartUIE-8K, a large-scale, user-centric evaluation dataset. ChartAJ provides rich AI feedback, empowering models to improve through context-tuning and test-time scaling without the need for costly training datasets. ChartUIE-8K offers diverse and realistic queries, with over 8,000 queries, a significant increase in chart types, topics, and underlying datasets, compared to existing works. Extensive double-blind studies confirm that both ChartAJ and ChartUIE-8K are state-of-the-art tools. Both contributions are open-source at chartaj.github.io, offering a substantial resource for future advancements in LLMs and chart generation.
